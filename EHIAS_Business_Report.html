Database Schema

RDBMS Architecture

The whole database is built around one idea: every patient event — the visit, the prescription, the lab test, the invoice — traces back to a single appointment row. Seven tables were extracted from a 30-column flat CSV and normalized into proper relational form.

Reading the diagram: Grey dashed arrows show the master hierarchy — departments contain doctors, and both doctors and patients flow into appointments. Blue dashed arrows show how prescriptions, bills, and lab reports each hang off a single appointment via foreign key. Gold PK = primary key; teal FK = foreign key.

How the Code Works

Procedures & Triggers

The database contains 9 stored procedures and 6 triggers that enforce business rules and generate reports without relying on application code. The original code had 3 bugs which are fixed here, highlighted in amber.

Triggers — 6 Total
TRIGGER — check_new_appointment (BEFORE INSERT)  ·  ✓ Fix 2 Applied
Runs on every new appointment insert. Blocks the row if the time is in the past or the doctor is already booked at that slot.
Conflict Prevention Data Integrity
1
No past appointments: Rejects if appointmenttime < NOW().
2
No double-booking: Checks if the same doctor already has a Scheduled appointment at that exact time.
TRIGGER — check_appointment_update (BEFORE UPDATE)
Mirrors the insert trigger but fires on updates. Prevents rescheduling an appointment to a past time and blocks double-booking when a doctor or time is changed.
Reschedule Guard Double-Book Check
TRIGGER — prevent_doctor_delete (BEFORE DELETE)
Blocks deletion of any doctor who still has future scheduled appointments. Protects referential integrity beyond what foreign keys alone enforce.
Referential Safety Staff Protection
TRIGGER — validate_patient_phone_insert / _update (BEFORE INSERT + UPDATE)
Validates that the patient phone number is exactly 10 numeric digits. Fires on both insert and update operations. Uses REGEXP to reject any non-digit characters.
Input Validation Data Quality
TRIGGER — auto_bill_on_completion (AFTER UPDATE)
When an appointment status changes to 'Completed', this trigger automatically creates a bill entry if one doesn't already exist. Ensures no completed visit goes unbilled.
Auto-Billing Revenue Assurance

Business Problems Solved

Why this database exists

Every design decision in this database was made to solve a real operational problem. Here are the eight core challenges the schema and its built-in logic address directly.

01
Scheduling Conflicts & Double-Booking
Without a guard, two patients could be booked with the same doctor at the exact same time. Two triggers — one on INSERT, one on UPDATE — ensure no double-booking can happen at the data layer, even when rescheduling.
Solved by check_new_appointment + check_appointment_update triggers
02
Patient Data Privacy & Clinical Access Control
With 200 doctors across 10 departments, you can't have everyone seeing everyone's patient records. The view_doctor_data procedure enforces role-based access at the database layer — Senior sees the department, others see only their own patients.
Solved by the role-gated stored procedure with credential authentication
03
₹27.70 Lakh in Outstanding Payments
Nearly 31% of all billed revenue is unpaid. The vw_unpaid_bills view, sp_outstanding_bills procedure, and the highest-balance analytical query make tracking overdue amounts a one-line operation for the billing team.
Solved via vw_unpaid_bills view + sp_outstanding_bills procedure + analytical queries
04
Missed Billing on Completed Appointments
When an appointment is marked Completed, a bill must exist. The auto_bill_on_completion trigger automatically generates a bill entry the moment the status changes — no manual intervention needed, no visit goes unbilled.
Solved by the AFTER UPDATE trigger — auto-generates bill on status change to Completed
05
Data Quality — Invalid Phone Numbers
Patient phone numbers must be exactly 10 numeric digits. Two validation triggers on the patients table — one for INSERT, one for UPDATE — reject any entry that doesn't meet this format using REGEXP validation.
Solved by validate_patient_phone_insert and _update triggers with REGEXP check
06
Accidental Doctor Deletion
Deleting a doctor who has upcoming scheduled appointments would orphan those records. The prevent_doctor_delete trigger blocks this at the database level — the doctor record stays until all future appointments are resolved.
Solved by the BEFORE DELETE trigger on doctors table
07
Reconstructing a Patient's Full Clinical Journey
When a patient returns for a follow-up, the doctor needs the full picture. The sp_patient_history procedure returns all appointments, prescriptions, lab results, and bills for any patient in a single call.
Solved by sp_patient_history procedure + appointment-centric hub schema
08
Critical Lab Value Detection
Lab reports store WBC, RBC, and Hemoglobin as JSON. The sp_abnormal_lab_reports procedure parses these values and flags any patient with readings outside normal ranges — enabling early clinical intervention.
Solved by sp_abnormal_lab_reports with JSON_EXTRACT and range-based flagging
